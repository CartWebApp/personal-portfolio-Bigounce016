Year in Review

If I had to describe this year, I would say it was an epic journey that led me to explore new and wonderful things and new opportunities. Working with everyone I worked with and learning how to write better, think critically, and work with others taught me so much — and I have been so happy to do all I have done. With my instructors and my peers, everything has been wonderful.

Where I started was nowhere. I had a little knowledge of coding which I came to learn didn't really help me, as I didn't remember much of it. I learned from the beginning that it wasn't gonna be easy and that I was gonna face very difficult challenges throughout the school year. I was able to move along though with the help from others and I learned many new things — from looking at a blank file to filling it with anything I could want. I had so many good ideas and had ways to actually implement them.

When it came to working with others, I learned that collaboration was a part of the class and actually helped me make many friends. It taught me how to communicate my ideas clearly and not to shoot down anyone's ideas for seeming dumb, but instead lead them to an even better idea to benefit everyone.

When we started to code, I was lost and tried my best to keep up — and I asked every question I possibly could have. Which was honestly a great idea, and it led to great moments where I said I was dumb because I was overthinking a not so complicated problem that truly had an easy fix. Mr. Fast gave me moments where I would just go "Oh I get it now" — mostly when I was learning how to use JavaScript and how to create cool functional sites.

Now with everything I know, I know I can effectively create a functioning site with professional skills in web development. I can now create awesome animations for sites using CSS, and it helps me make cool little details that look nice. I also learned how to use APIs and various different JavaScript things that added major functionality to my sites. I am grateful to have done what I have done so far.

Five Year Plan

My plan for the next few years of my life is still just a bunch of learning. I want to advance my skills further and go into computer science, which will teach me more than just web development.

Year One

My first year out of high school is community college — learning my basic GED classes and understanding what adulthood can now offer me. I have many opportunities ahead and I plan to take them all. I will get a job, hopefully, and have something stable that can work with school. I also want to get my driver's license so that I don't have to be taken everywhere.

Year Two

My second year of college will be like the first — working and going to school every day — but I have to plan to go to a better college after I'm done with community, since I will only be there for two years. I have to plan to move out of my house and learn to live on my own, be self-sufficient, and not depend on others. I will find out where I am going to live and where I could possibly work, and figure out how it is all going to work with school.

Year Three

Year three is the first hard year where I am all by myself and off on new adventures and places to explore. I will be going to a very good college for my major and it'll help me learn what life is like on your own. I have to keep up with school and make sure I get my work done. I have to make sure not to slack off in class or I will fall behind. I have to make sure that I don't recklessly spend all my money and I should learn to budget.

Year Four

My fourth year of college is just like the last but I know what I'm doing now and have gotten into a good routine that'll keep me going. I'll miss my family and work hard so that I can go see them during breaks for school. I'll work hard in classes and keep working towards my goal, and I'll make many new friends who will help keep me on track but will also let me know it is okay to relax sometimes. I also will keep up with my budgeting and will make sure that I don't run out of money.

Year Five

Year 5 is me still working towards my goal of getting my master's in computer science. One more year after this and I'm done. I'll reflect on how much I've done so far and how far I've come since the beginning of college. I'll look back on all the adventures I've been on and look forward to what is next to come. I'll also get to see my family more often after that too. I'll keep up in school and I will never slack off and get lost. I will always remember to be me.
